Precautionary Principle Statement

Direct Planetary Cooling is presented as a personal hypothesis and precautionary proposal, not as an absolute prediction. Its purpose is preparedness: to consider a possible climate-risk scenario involving weakened carbon sinks, biological circulation, ocean circulation, soil systems, microbial cycles, water cycles, natural cooling functions, and thermal inertia.

Core Position

This framework does not replace emissions reduction. It argues that emissions reduction should be combined with carbon sink restoration, water-cycle recovery, ecosystem regeneration, soil and microbial recovery, ocean circulation support, and nature-compatible cooling support.

Risk Logic

If the hypothesis is overestimated, many preparations remain beneficial: healthier soils, stronger forests, improved water cycles, stronger carbon sinks, cooler cities, better disaster resilience, and improved ecological monitoring. If the risk is underestimated, delayed preparation may leave society with fewer options.
